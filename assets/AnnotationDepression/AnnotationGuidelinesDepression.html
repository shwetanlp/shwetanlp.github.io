Depression Symptom Identification Annotation Guidelines

Is Tweet Depressive?: It has two options (“Yes” and “No”)

A tweet can be categorized in a depression (Yes) category if it explicitly or implicitly describes one or many symptoms such as:

  1. Loss of interest: Loss of interest or pleasure in most or all normal activities, such as sex, hobbies, or sports.
  2. Feeling Down: Feelings of sadness, tearfulness, emptiness, or hopelessness.
  3. Sleeping Disorder: Sleep disturbances, including insomnia, sleeping too much, or trouble falling or staying asleep.
  4. Lack of Energy: Tiredness and lack of energy, so even small tasks take extra effort.
  5. Eating Disorder: Reduced appetite and weight loss or increased cravings for food and weight gain.
  6. Low Self-Esteem: Feelings of worthlessness or guilt, fixating on past failures or self-blame.
  7. Concentration Problem: Trouble thinking, concentrating, making decisions, and remembering things.
  8. Hyper/Lower Activity: Moving or speaking so slowly that other people could have noticed? Or the opposite - being so fidgety or restless that you have been moving around a lot more than usual?
  9. Self-Harm: Frequent or recurrent thoughts of death, suicidal thoughts, suicide attempts, or suicide.

Select “NO” If it does not have any of the above symptoms mentioned explicitly or implicitly.

Is a tweet figurative? Contain two options (“Yes” and “No”)

  • Please select “Yes” if a tweet does not contain literal meaning but implicitly describes something by comparing it to something else. It uses metaphors, sarcasm, similes, and other examples to help illustrate the object you are talking about.
  • Please select “No” otherwise.

If Selected “Yes”

  • If you have selected “Yes” for the “Is tweet figurative? ”, you need to choose what type of figurative language class it could belong to. You could select “Sarcasm”, “Metaphor”, or “Other”.

Definition and Examples:

  1. Sarcasm: A tweet can be defined as sarcastic, where the use of words that mean the opposite of what you really want to say, especially in order to insult someone, to show irritation, or to be funny

Example 1: “ i want a zombie to read my nutrition label, be happy to see it say low fat , and then eat me” (shows depression symptom: Eating disorder)

  1. Metaphor: A tweet in which a word or phrase literally denoting one kind of object or idea is used in place of another to suggest a likeness or analogy between them.

Example 2: “My skin is paper, razor is the pen” (shows depression symptom: Self Harm)
